# Field Visit Checklist

Use this checklist when visiting a candidate water tower, standpipe, tank, or
public water storage landmark. The goal is public-interest documentation from
lawful public viewpoints, not site access.

## Before You Go

- [ ] Confirm the candidate location from public maps, OpenStreetMap, city
      documents, or other public sources.
- [ ] Check whether the site appears to be visible from a public road, sidewalk,
      trail, park, or other lawful public place.
- [ ] Review the weather, daylight, parking, and traffic conditions.
- [ ] Bring a charged phone or camera.
- [ ] Bring a notebook or notes app.
- [ ] Avoid planning any route that crosses fences, gates, private property,
      utility property, rail corridors, or restricted areas.

## Safety And Boundaries

- [ ] Stay in lawful public areas.
- [ ] Do not trespass, climb, enter, open, move, touch, or test anything.
- [ ] Do not photograph or document gate codes, locks, cameras, alarms, access
      panels, control cabinets, SCADA/control equipment, internal piping, or
      security signage.
- [ ] If sensitive details appear in a wide photo, crop or blur them before
      publishing.
- [ ] Do not interfere with workers, vehicles, emergency access, or utility
      operations.
- [ ] Leave immediately if asked by lawful authority or if the situation feels
      unsafe.

## Field Observations To Capture

- [ ] Date and local time.
- [ ] Approximate coordinates from a public viewpoint.
- [ ] Nearest public intersection or landmark.
- [ ] Whether the site is visible from public space.
- [ ] Structure category: elevated tank, standpipe, ground tank, reservoir,
      hydropneumatic tank, unknown.
- [ ] Visible material or exterior type: steel, concrete, composite, wood,
      unknown.
- [ ] Visible color/coating.
- [ ] Visible name, logo, city, district, or public-facing label.
- [ ] General setting: neighborhood, industrial area, hilltop, park, municipal
      facility, rural road, etc.
- [ ] General condition from public view only: newly painted, weathered,
      under construction, apparently inactive, demolished, unknown.
- [ ] Public signs that are civic/non-sensitive, such as a water district name.
- [ ] Any nearby public project signs or bid/construction signs.

## Photos

- [ ] Take a wide overview photo from a public viewpoint.
- [ ] Take a second photo from another public angle if available.
- [ ] Capture enough surrounding public context to identify the structure.
- [ ] Avoid close-ups of security, access, controls, telecom/RF equipment, or
      operational details.
- [ ] Record photo direction or viewpoint in notes when useful.
- [ ] Review photos before publishing and crop/blur sensitive details.

## Research Follow-Up

- [ ] Search city, county, district, and utility websites.
- [ ] Search council packets, minutes, bid notices, legal notices, master plans,
      and capital improvement plans.
- [ ] Search state/EPA public water system records when relevant.
- [ ] Search OpenStreetMap and add the OSM object ID if useful.
- [ ] Record source URLs, archive URLs, access dates, and publication dates.
- [ ] Extract claims as source-backed facts rather than unsourced prose.
- [ ] Mark uncertain facts as `unknown`, `approximate`, or `needs verification`.

## Ready To Submit

- [ ] The location is represented as a point, not an access guide.
- [ ] Photos are from public viewpoints and reviewed for sensitive details.
- [ ] Claims have source IDs or are clearly marked as field observations.
- [ ] No restricted documents, leaked material, access instructions, or
      operational/security details are included.
- [ ] Open questions are listed for future contributors.
